Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup

A traditional taiwanese/chinese dish! This is one of my favorite noodle dishes which can be cooked in a slow cooker. One bowl of this on a cold day will warm you up immediately!

Nutrition Info

Meal prep: 25 minutes

Cooking time: 8 hours 15 minutes

Total time: 8 hours 40 minutes

Servings: 8

Yield: 8 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef shanks, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • Water to cover
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, or more if needed
  • 8 cups water, or more if needed
  • 1(14 ounce) can beef broth
  • 1 cups soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup rice wine
  • 1 bunch green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 10 gloves garlic, peeled, or more to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chile paste
  • 1 (1 1/2 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into 5 pieces
  • 3 star anise pods, or more to taste
  • 1 teaspoons Chinese five-spice powder
  • 4 small heads baby bok choy
  • 1 (10 ounce) package udon noodles
  • 1 tablespoon chopped pickled Chinese mustard greens, or to taste

Steps

  1. Place beef shanks in a stockpot and pour in water to cover; bring to a boil. Remove from heat and drain.
  2. Pour vegetable oil into a slow cooker; add beef, 8 cups water, beef broth, soy sauce, rice wine, green onions, brown sugar, garlic, chile peppers, chile paste, ginger, star anise, annd Chinese five-spice powder.
  3. Simmer on low for 8 to 9 hours.
  4. Transfer beef to a bowl with a slotted spoon. Strain broth into a bowl and discard solids.
  5. Bring a pot of water, add bok choy for 30 seconds. Remove and run under cold water. Cook noodles in the boiling water until tender but firm, about 4 minutes, than drain.
  6. Divide noodles among serving bowls and top with beef, baby bok choy, and broth. Top with chinese pickled mustard greens. Add more chili paste if you like it spicy!
